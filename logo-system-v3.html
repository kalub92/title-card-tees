The Primary Mark

One horizontal wordmark. One confident squiggle. The Memphis DNA lives in the line, not in the letters — which is what makes it wearable and keeps it elevated.

01 Construction

Three moves. Nothing else.

The mark is built on restraint. No triangle dot on the "i". No floating Memphis shapes in the lockup. Three decisions carry the whole identity.

01

Typography

Archivo Black at full caps, letter-spacing tightened to −2 (optical). Letters bear weight; the mark reads from across a room and on a woven tag.

02

The Signature

A six-wave squiggle at .45× cap-height distance below baseline. Stroke weight equal to .09× cap-height. Not a flourish — a signed line.

03

Restraint

No internal color breaks, no altered letters, no floating shapes in the logo lockup. Memphis energy is carried entirely by the squiggle.

02 The Signature

Independent of the wordmark, the squiggle can stand alone — as a pattern, a divider, a social-post motif. It's the brand's smallest possible expression and reappears throughout the site, ticker, hangtag, and packaging.

03 System Variants

One mark, three lockups.

Primary is horizontal. When space is square, stack. When space is a favicon, switch to the monogram inside the card frame — the brand's smallest form that still reads as TCT.

Primary — Horizontal
Default lockup. Use whenever horizontal space allows. Site headers, hero graphics, garment hem prints.
Stacked — Square
Three-line stack with the squiggle still as signature. Profile pictures, square banners, label tags.
Monogram — Icon
For 16-pixel applications: favicon, app icon, social notification, hangtag seal, big footer sign-off.

05 Clear Space + Minimum Size

Give it room. Don't shrink it.

The mark needs the breathing room of the letter "T" on all sides. Below 120px wide in digital or 1.25in in print, switch to the monogram — the squiggle gets lost at small sizes.

Clear Space

Padding equal to the height of a capital "T" on all four sides. Nothing enters the zone — not copy, not icons, not other marks.

USE MONOGRAM

Minimum Size

Horizontal mark: 120px wide / 1.25in wide. Below that, switch to the monogram lockup — squiggle stays legible inside the card frame.

06 Misuse

Don't do these.

The mark is simple enough to break easily. Six violations show up most often — each one quietly erodes the identity. If you're looking at a version of the logo that resembles any of these, stop.

✗
Don't stretch the wordmark

Archivo Black's proportions are tuned. Horizontal or vertical scaling distorts the strokes and kills the confidence.

✗
Don't recolor individual letters

The wordmark is one color. Multi-color letters fragment the mark and make it read as decoration, not identity.

✗
Don't float the squiggle

The squiggle sits .45× cap-height below baseline. Above the wordmark, detached, or orbiting — all wrong. It's a signature, not a garnish.

✗
Don't pair two pops

One neutral (Ink or Cream) carries the wordmark; one pop carries the squiggle. Pop-on-pop clashes and reads as an error.

✗
Don't place on a pop background

The mark lives on Cream or Ink — full stop. Teal, Rose, Mustard, and Tangerine are backgrounds for graphics, not for the logo.

✗
Don't decorate the lockup

No floating triangles, dots, or arcs inside the clear space. The Memphis energy lives in the squiggle. Accents belong in surrounding graphics.
