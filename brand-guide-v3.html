TCT is not a costume shop. Not Hot Topic. Not Spencer's. Not a throwback brand shouting remember this?. It's elevated nostalgia for the people who lived it — rendered with the restraint of adulthood and the confidence of a design house.

02 The Drop Model

One drop at a time.

TCT doesn't have a catalog. It has a broadcast schedule. One drop is live at any moment; the next drop has a date. When a piece is gone, it's gone. This is the operating principle the whole brand hangs from.

01

One drop live.

The store shows only the current drop. No browse-and-compare against past collections.

02

250 per piece.

Every piece in every drop is limited to 250 units. Numbered by hand on the hem tag.

03

No restocks.

When it's gone, it's gone. Designs are retired on sellout. The archive lives with customers.

04

Public calendar.

Next drop date is always visible. Drops are events. Customers plan around them.

03 Site Experience

What you see is what's on.

The site enforces the drop model. One drop is visible; the next drop's date is stated. Nothing else exists on titlecardtees.com — no past collections, no browsable archive, no "here's what we used to make." Absence is the feature.

01

Only the current drop is shoppable.

When you land on the site, you see what's airing now. No other drops exist as pages. No collections menu, no shop index, no filter-by-type.

02

The next drop has a countdown.

A dedicated section names the next drop, shows the airdate, and counts down in real time. One-tap email capture for a single drop-day notification.

03

No archive. Ever.

When a drop ends, it disappears. The product pages, the photos, the copy — retired. New customers cannot browse past drops. The archive lives with customers, in their closets.

04

Sold-out is in-place.

When a piece in the live drop sells out, its card stays visible with a SOLD OUT overlay — it proves the drop is real. When all six pieces sell, the site transitions to Next Drop mode.

04 The Logo

Wordmark plus signature.

The mark is a horizontal wordmark set in Archivo Black with a single six-wave squiggle as signature. No floating accents in the lockup — the Memphis energy lives in the line. See the Logo System document for construction, variants, clear space, and mockups.

05 Color Palette

Six colors, used with discipline.

Cream and Ink carry the brand — roughly 80% of touchpoints. The four pops (Tangerine, Teal, Rose, Mustard) are used one-at-a-time except on Memphis hero graphics where three colors can compose. Tangerine is the accent; it runs the squiggle, the dots in the ticker, the CTAs.

07 Voice & Tone

Don't explain the joke.

The voice is someone who was there and grew up — not someone cosplaying nostalgia. Confident, understated, specific. Trusts the reader to catch references without footnotes. Never uses "throwback," "vibes," "iykyk," or emoji.

08 Naming System

Name like a librarian.

Two tiers. Drops (what used to be called collections) reference the rituals of the era — when or where cartoons were consumed. Pieces reference the artifacts of the era. Every name is a noun lifted from lived 90s experience. No dates, no "throwback," no "retro."

Drops

Format: [Ritual or Moment] — 1–2 words, no punctuation.

  • Saturday MorningDrop 01 — launch, the flagship ritual
  • After SchoolDrop 02 — 3 PM to 5 PM energy
  • Basic Cable60-channel stretch, Nick at Nite era
  • Blockbuster NightFriday rental-night pieces
  • Sunday FunniesComic-strip influenced
  • Snow DayWinter holiday drop

Pieces

Format: The [Cultural Object] — always definite article, always singular.

  • The BowlBreakfast-cereal color-blocked tee
